What is PanDrugs?

PanDrugs provides a bioinformatics platform to prioritize anticancer drug treatments according to individual multi-omics data. The current version of PanDrugs database integrates data from 23 primary sources and stores 74,087 drug-target associations obtained from 4,642 genes and 14,659 unique compounds.

Data input:

  1. VCF file: for somatic and germline variant analysis.
  2. RNK file: for pre-ranked gene lists.
  3. TSV file: for CNV analysis.
  4. Gene lists.
  5. Drug queries.

Please note PanDrugs terminology for druggable genes:

  1. Direct targets: Genes that contribute to a disease phenotype and can be directly targeted by a drug (e.g. BRAF is a direct target of vemurafenib).
  2. Biomarkers: Genes whose genetic status is associated with a drug response but are not the drug target themselves (e.g. BRCA-mutated cancers responding to PARP inhibitors).
  3. Genetic dependencies: Druggable genes upon which an altered gene fuctionally depends (e.g. patients with BRAF GoF treated with BRAF and MEK inhibitors can develop resistance involving MAPK signaling reactivation. Thus, PanDrugs suggests targeting MAP2K1 in tumors with BRAF GoF).
  4. Pathway members: Druggable genes found within the pathway and downstream of an altered gene (e.g. patients with mutations in TSC1/2 respond to downstream inhibition of the mTOR pathway).

PanDrugs workflow

PanDrugs considers drug indication and approval status, the number of genes associated with a drug, the nature and reliability of these associations and the type of drug response to calculate the Drug Score (DScore). The Gene Score (GScore) is estimated according to gene essentiality, tumour vulnerability, the relevance of the gene in cancer, its druggability level, the biological impact of mutations, the frequency of gene alterations and their clinical implications. PanDrugs resolves the Best Therapeutic Candidates (BTC) based on both the DScore and the GScore.
